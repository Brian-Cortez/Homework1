Projects

I have worked on several game development projects that have helped me improve my programming and design skills. My projects include a multiplayer air hockey game, a virtual reality stealth horror game, and a restaurant simulation game. Through these projects, I have gained experience with Unity, C#, artificial intelligence, multiplayer networking, user interface development, debugging, and gameplay system design.

Multiplayer Air Hockey

Multiplayer Air Hockey is a Unity game in which two players compete by controlling paddles and attempting to hit a physics-based puck into the opposing goal. I developed the player movement, scoring system, puck behavior, user interface, and match-reset system.

I also worked with Unity Netcode to create server-authoritative multiplayer movement and synchronize information between connected players. This project helped me improve my skills in Unity physics, networking, remote procedure calls, debugging, and multiplayer game development.

VR Stealth Horror Game

My VR stealth horror project features a fisherman enemy that can wander around the environment, detect the player, chase them, and attack when they are close enough. I created the enemy's behavior states, detection system, traps, movement, and attack mechanics.

The project also includes player sprinting, crouching, virtual reality movement, and immersive sound effects. Working on this game strengthened my understanding of artificial intelligence, state-based programming, navigation, player interaction, and the Unity XR Interaction Toolkit.

Restaurant Simulation Game

The Restaurant Simulation Game allows players to prepare food and serve NPC customers. Customers enter the restaurant, place orders, wait for service, sit at tables, eat their meals, and leave after they finish.

I developed the customer AI states, food-ordering system, cooking minigames, ticket queue interface, object-pooling system, and save-and-load features. This project gave me experience with event-driven programming, data management, navigation, user interfaces, and connecting multiple gameplay systems together.
